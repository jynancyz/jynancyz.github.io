Interview Study

i

A research project done at UCSD's DesignLab under Professor Steven Dow.

Goal: conduct interviews with civic leaders to understand the workings of civic engagement in the real world.

What I did

♦   Constructed interview questions concerning status of civic engagement
♦   Conducted interviews with civic leaders
♦   Collected qualitative interview data in the form of audio files, written notes
♦   Composed publication based on refined results of interviewing with the use of LaTeX/Overleaf

&

What I learned

♦   Biases must be taken into account when the subject's position is considered in the research topic.
♦   In-situ observation is crucial to obtaining a clear idea on how civic events work.
♦   Solutions can introduce new problems; wicked problems plague the civic sphere.
♦   Inclusivity allows for more insights; more interviews needed to be conducted to capture experience from eventgoers' point of view.



The Process:

1. Literature Review: Learning More on Existing Issues/Solutions

Most of papers that were read and evaluated came from the ACM Digital Library. We focused on the previous years's CHI and CSCW papers. Through literature review, I scoped out methods that have been investigated in the civic sphere focusing on methods which include civic engagement online. While many papers describe the benefits of using technology to engage the public, there were also various issues that were raised such as a lack of moderation.



2. Interviews Conducted to Better Understand Civic Sphere

Example questions: "How are you involved in civic engagement?",
"What do you think of the civic engagement processes that are currently used?"



3. Qualitative Data Categorized Into Main Themes

We found that most civic issues circle around three key elements: Scale, Transparency, and Convergence. These were supported by transcribed quotes from the conducted interviews with civic leaders and planners. The data was split into three categories concerning: Participation, Retention, and Openness of Process which line up with the civic issues found.



4. Paper writing!

Along with two other team members, I contributed a total of five written drafts per section. These were re-iterated upon after discussion with the professor. These drafts were later inputted into LaTex and references were added to complete the draft.
